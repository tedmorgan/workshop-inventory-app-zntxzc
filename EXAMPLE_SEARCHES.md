### Example 4: Sanding Project
**Your Question:**
```
I need to sand a wooden table. What should I use?
```

**AI Response:**
```
For sanding a wooden table, here's what I recommend:

1. **Orbital Sander** - Bin C, Garage Wall
   Perfect for: Smooth, even sanding of flat surfaces like your 
   table top. Much faster than hand sanding.

2. **Sandpaper (various grits)** - Bin D, Tool Chest
   You'll need: Start with 80-grit for rough spots, then 120-grit 
   for smoothing, and finish with 220-grit for a silky finish.

3. **Sanding Block** - Bin A, Shelf 3
   Useful for: Edges and corners where the orbital sander can't reach.

Process:
1. Start with coarse grit (80) to remove old finish
2. Progress to medium grit (120) for smoothing
3. Finish with fine grit (220) for a smooth surface
4. Always sand with the grain, not against it

Safety: Wear a dust mask and work in a well-ventilated area!
```
